Olympic History

The Olympic Website

Ancient Olympics (776 BC – 393 AD)

  • The first olympic games were held in 776 BC in Greece, to honor Zeus.
  • The games took place every 4 years and featured events such as running, wrestling, boxing and chariot racing.
  • Only free greek men were alowed to compete.
  • An olympic truce paused wars during the games.
  • The ancient Olympics ended in 393 CE when they were banned by Roman Emperor Theodosius I.

The Revival of the Olympics (19th Century)

  • Interest in the Olympics returned in the 1800s.
  • Baron Pierre de Coubertin promoted the idea of international sport for peace and education.
  • In 1894, the International Olympic Committee (IOC) was founded.
  • The first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens in 1896.

Modern Olympic Games (1896 – Present)

  • The first summer olympic games was in 1896 with 14 nations and 241 athletes.
  • Women were first allowed to compete in 1900.
  • The first winter olympic games was in 1924 in France, focusing on ice and snow sports.
  • The games were expanded to include 200+ countries and 30+ sports.
  • The Olympic flame and torch relay became important traditions.
